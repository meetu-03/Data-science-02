# What are LAN, MAN, and WAN?

LAN, MAN, and WAN are types of computer networks classified according to their geographical coverage area.

## 1. LAN (Local Area Network)

### Definition
A LAN is a network that connects computers and devices within a small area such as a home, office, school, or laboratory.

### Example
- Computers connected in a school computer lab.
- Devices connected through a home Wi-Fi network.

### Advantages
- High data transfer speed
- Low cost
- Easy maintenance
- Secure communication

---

## 2. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

### Definition
A MAN is a network that covers a city or a large campus by connecting multiple LANs.

### Example
- A university connecting different buildings across a city.
- A bank connecting all its branches within a city.

### Advantages
- Covers a larger area than LAN
- Faster communication within a city
- Efficient resource sharing

---

## 3. WAN (Wide Area Network)

### Definition
A WAN is a network that covers a very large geographical area such as countries or continents.

### Example
- The Internet
- A multinational company connecting offices worldwide

### Advantages
- Global connectivity
- Remote access to data
- Easy communication across countries

---

## Comparison Table

| Feature | LAN | MAN | WAN |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Full Form | Local Area Network | Metropolitan Area Network | Wide Area Network |
| Coverage Area | Small (Building/Home) | City | Country/World |
| Speed | High | Medium | Lower than LAN |
| Cost | Low | Medium | High |
| Example | School Lab | City-wide Bank Network | Internet |

## Diagram

```text
LAN  --> Home / Office Network

          (Internet)
```

## Conclusion

- **LAN** connects devices within a small area.
- **MAN** connects networks across a city.
- **WAN** connects networks across countries and continents.

The Internet is the best example of a **WAN**.
